## 1. Scalable Optimization of Ultra-Dense Heterogeneous Networks Using Stochastic Geometry and Deep Learning Techniques
- **Authors:** Amna Shabbir, **Muhammad Hashir Bin Khalid**, Hashim Raza Khan, Kamran Arshad, Khaled Assaleh
- **Venue:** *AI* (MDPI), vol. 7, no. 2, art. 76 — 2026/2/15
- **URL:** https://www.mdpi.com/2673-2688/7/2/76
- **Tags:** Deep Learning, Wireless, Energy Efficiency
- **Key result:** Combines stochastic geometry with deep learning to optimize energy efficiency in ultra-dense 5G networks under practical QoS constraints.
- **Abstract:** Ultra-dense networks (UDNs) enable next-generation wireless systems by providing high capacity through aggressive base-station densification. However, dense deployments increase interference and energy consumption, making Quality-of-Service (QoS) aware performance evaluation and optimization challenging. Stochastic geometry (SG) provides a tractable framework for modeling large-scale UDNs, but its use is often limited by simplifying assumptions and simulation requirements. In parallel, Deep Learning (DL) offers scalable tools for capturing complex network behavior from data. This paper proposes a scalable analytical and data-driven framework for performance evaluation and energy efficiency (EE) optimization in UDNs. SG-based analysis is used to derive expressions for key metrics, including coverage probability and EE, under practical QoS constraints such as base-station density and transmit power.

## 2. Fuzzy MRAS Speed Sensorless Induction Motor Drive Control for Electric Vehicles
- **Authors:** Saqib Jamshed Rind, Saba Javed, Hashim Raza Khan, **Muhammad Hashir Bin Khalid**, Kamran Arshad, Khaled Assaleh
- **Venue:** *Energies* (MDPI), vol. 19, no. 6, art. 1580 — 2026/3/23
- **URL:** https://www.mdpi.com/1996-1073/19/6/1580
- **Tags:** EV, Motor Control, Fuzzy Logic
- **Key result:** A fuzzy-logic MRAS observer for sensorless induction-motor speed estimation in EVs — robust to load-torque disturbances, replacing conventional constant-gain PI.
- **Abstract:** This paper proposes a new fuzzy logic-based rotor flux model reference adaptive system (FLC-MRAS) for rotor speed estimation in induction motor drives, replacing the constant-gain PI controller used in conventional MRAS schemes. The proposed observer simultaneously incorporates both rotor flux and electromagnetic torque errors to enhance estimation accuracy and robustness against load torque disturbances. A nonlinear Mamdani-type fuzzy logic controller (FLC) with two inputs and one output, employing triangular membership functions and seven fuzzy sets, is adopted. The effectiveness is examined through extensive simulation cases under various vehicle speed driving profiles and load torque conditions using an indirect vector-controlled induction motor drive.

## 3. Design and Development of a Configurable High Voltage Power Supply for Atmospheric Plasma Generation
- **Authors:** Taha Amjad, **Muhammad Hashir Bin Khalid**, Mubbshir Saleem, Hashim Raza Khan, Kamran Arshad, Khaled Assaleh
- **Venue:** *2026 1st International Conference on Emerging Technologies and Engineering Systems (ICETES)* (IEEE), pp. 348–353 — 2026/4/7
- **URL:** https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/abstract/document/11518598
- **Tags:** Power Electronics, Embedded, High Voltage
- **Key result:** STM32-controlled push-pull inverter delivering an adjustable output up to ~20 kV from 24–30 VDC for atmospheric plasma loads, with opto-isolated drivers and OV/OC protection.
- **Abstract:** The design and experimental validation of a low-power, high-voltage power supply for atmospheric plasma generation are presented. The proposed system is based on a MOSFET-based push-pull inverter topology that drives a center-tapped step-up transformer to convert a 24–30 VDC input into an adjustable high-voltage output up to approximately 20 kV suitable for atmospheric-pressure plasma loads. The inverter operates in the 19–23 kHz range, with the STM32 microcontroller generating complementary PWM gate signals that have configurable duty cycles, frequencies, and dead times to control the output voltage and power delivered to the plasma reactor. The power stage integrates optically isolated MOSFET gate drivers, dedicated voltage and current sensing circuits, and comparator-based overvoltage and overcurrent protection. A Nextion HMI touchscreen provides a user-friendly interface.

## 4. A Low-Cost Energy Monitoring System with Universal Compatibility and Real-Time Visualization for Enhanced Accessibility and Power Savings
- **Authors:** Hashim Raza Khan, Majida Kazmi, Lubaba, **Muhammad Hashir Bin Khalid**, Urooj Alam, Kamran Arshad, Khaled Assaleh, Saad Ahmed Qazi
- **Venue:** *Sustainability* (MDPI), vol. 16, no. 10, art. 4137 — 2024/5/15
- **URL:** https://www.mdpi.com/2071-1050/16/10/4137
- **Tags:** IoT, Energy Monitoring, Embedded
- **Cited by:** 15 (as of Jun 2026)
- **Key result:** A low-cost, universally-compatible IoT energy monitoring system with a secure real-time dashboard — about 40% cheaper to build than comparable systems.
- **Abstract:** Energy management is important for both consumers and utility providers. Utility providers are concerned with identifying and reducing energy wastage and thefts; consumers are interested in reducing their energy consumption and bills. In Pakistan, residential and industrial estates account for nearly 31,000 MW of the maximum total demand, while the transmission and distribution capacity has stalled at about 22,000 MW. This gap has led to frequent load shedding, and the aging transmission and distribution network cannot deliver the requisite power at all times. Several energy monitoring systems (EnMS) have been proposed, but they face limitations in cost, ease of application, universal installation capability, customization, and data security. This work develops an economical, secure, and customizable real-time EnMS comprising low-cost hardware with universal compatibility, a secured communication module for real-time data transmission, and a dashboard application for visualization. Experimental results revealed approximately 40% cost savings in EnMS development compared to other systems.

## 5. Dataset of Usage Pattern and Energy Analysis of an Internet of Things-Enabled Ceiling Fan
- **Authors:** Hashim Raza Khan, **Muhammad Hashir bin Khalid**, Urooj Alam, Mahnoor Atiq, Uvais Qidwai, Saad Ahmed Qazi
- **Venue:** *Data in Brief* (Elsevier), vol. 46, art. 108900 — 2023/2/1
- **URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2352340923000185
- **Tags:** IoT, Dataset, Energy
- **Cited by:** 6 (as of Jun 2026)
- **Key result:** An open dataset logging BLDC ceiling-fan usage (speed) with temperature and humidity to a cloud server over Wi-Fi, enabling energy-pattern analysis.
- **Abstract:** Many electrical appliances have progressed from prototypes to viable products by being automated with sensors and the Internet of Things (IoT). There are few data-centric solutions for the effective use of residential and commercial ceiling fans. Sensors were installed on a remote-controlled BLDC ceiling fan, and a large amount of user data with environmental indicators such as temperature and humidity was collected. This data, along with the fan speed, was logged to a cloud server over Wi-Fi using a Wi-Fi-enabled microcontroller. The raw data consists of timestamp, temperature, humidity, and fan speed. The data is logged on the change of any parameter rather than at a specific interval, then visualized on the cloud server to monitor usage patterns and subsequent energy consumption.

## 6. Threefold Optimized Forecasting of Electricity Consumption in Higher Education Institutions
- **Authors:** Majida Kazmi, Hashim Raza Khan, **Mohammad Hashir Bin Khalid**, Saad Ahmed Qazi
- **Venue:** *Computers, Materials & Continua*, vol. 73, no. 2 — 2022/11/1
- **URL:** https://openurl.ebsco.com/EPDB%3Agcd%3A13%3A33440989/detailv2?sid=ebsco%3Aplink%3Ascholar&id=ebsco%3Agcd%3A157555155&crl=c&link_origin=scholar.google.com
- **Tags:** Machine Learning, Forecasting, Energy
- **Cited by:** 4 (as of Jun 2026)
- **Key result:** Compares back-propagation ANN vs Random Forest for building electricity forecasting; RF achieves the best accuracy/speed trade-off — MAPE 2.42% in 11.1 s at 1-min granularity.
- **Abstract:** Energy management benefits both consumers and utility companies. A large supply-demand gap of over 6 GW existed in Pakistan as reported in 2018; efficient demand-side energy management has the potential to reduce this gap economically. Electricity load forecasting models are increasingly used by energy managers for real-time tactical decisions. Machine-learning (ML) technology has enabled accurate forecasting, but the computation cost of these models is often ignored in favour of accuracy. This study considers both accuracy and computation cost as concurrently significant. A three-fold optimized load forecasting model is proposed: (1) application-specific parameter selection, (2) impact of different dataset granularities, and (3) specific data preparation. It compares back-propagation Artificial Neural Network (ANN) and Random Forest (RF) for predicting university building electricity consumption, embedding weather data and university operational calendars as inputs. The RF model performed better in accuracy and computational time across 1-min, 15-min, and 1-h granularities — MAPE of 2.42, 3.70, and 4.62 in 11.1 s, 1.14 s, and 0.3 s respectively — well suited for real-time energy monitoring.

## 7. An Isolated Power Factor Corrected Cuk Converter with Integrated Magnetics for Brushless DC Ceiling Fan Applications
- **Authors:** Hashim Raza Khan, Majida Kazmi, Haris Bin Ashraf, **Muhammad Hashir Bin Khalid**, Abul Hasan, Saad Ahmed Qazi
- **Venue:** *Electronics* (MDPI), vol. 10, no. 14, art. 1720 — 2021/7/17
- **URL:** https://www.mdpi.com/2079-9292/10/14/1720
- **Tags:** Power Electronics, Motor Drives, PFC
- **Key result:** An isolated PFC Cuk converter with integrated magnetics for sensorless BLDC fan drives — reduces motor torque ripple to 2.14% while holding 0.999 power factor and 4.72% THD at full load.
- **Abstract:** The usage of BLDC motors in the low-power range is increasing rapidly in home appliances such as ceiling fans, necessitating reliable, compact, and efficient AC-DC power supplies for motor drive circuitry. This paper presents a power supply design consisting of an AC-DC isolated PFC Cuk converter with integrated magnetics that supplies a single-shunt voltage source inverter for the sensorless drive of the BLDC fan motor. The integrated magnetics structure has the two inductors and the transformer windings sharing the same core; zero input and output ripple current conditions are derived from the reluctance model. The Cuk converter operates in continuous conduction mode (CCM) using the current multiplier method, ensuring near-unity power factor and low THD, and providing over-current protection. The BLDC motor speed is controlled by field oriented control (FOC), enabling direct operation with alternate energy sources such as batteries and solar PV. The proposed supply reduces motor torque ripple to only 2.14% while maintaining a 0.999 power factor and only 4.72% THD at full load. Failure-mode analysis was performed through PLECS software simulations.
